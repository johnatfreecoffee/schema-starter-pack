Our 50-Point Comprehensive Roof Assessment

Roofing Materials Assessment +
  • Shingle condition (cracks, curling, missing pieces)
  • Material age and expected lifespan remaining
  • Granule loss and surface deterioration
  • Color consistency and fading patterns
  • Algae, moss, or biological growth
  • Manufacturing defects or installation errors
Structural Integrity +
  • Roof decking condition and support
  • Sagging or uneven roof planes
  • Ridge line straightness
  • Truss and rafter assessment (interior when accessible)
  • Weight-bearing capacity evaluation
  • Previous repair quality assessment
Water Management Systems +
  • Gutter condition and attachment
  • Downspout functionality and drainage
  • Gutter guards or leaf protection
  • Proper slope and water flow
  • Standing water or pooling issues
  • Overflow marks or staining
Flashing & Penetrations +
  • Chimney flashing integrity
  • Vent pipe boots and seals
  • Skylight flashing and condition
  • Valley flashing and waterproofing
  • Drip edge installation and condition
  • Step flashing at wall intersections
Ventilation Assessment +
  • Intake ventilation (soffit vents)
  • Exhaust ventilation (ridge/gable vents)
  • Proper airflow balance
  • Attic temperature and moisture levels
  • Insulation condition and coverage
  • Vapor barrier effectiveness
Interior Indicators +
  • Attic inspection for leaks
  • Water stains on ceilings
  • Interior moisture or mold signs
  • Insulation damage from leaks
  • Daylight visibility through roof
  • Proper decking attachment
Storm & Wind Damage +
  • Wind-lifted or loose shingles
  • Hail impact damage assessment
  • Debris damage evaluation
  • Fastener exposure or failure
  • Edge lifting or corner damage
  • Overall weather exposure effects
Code Compliance & Safety +
  • Current building code compliance
  • Fire resistance rating verification
  • Proper fastening patterns
  • Load distribution assessment
  • Hurricane strap installation (if applicable)
  • Safety hazard identification

After Inspection

How often should I have my roof inspected?

We recommend annual professional inspections for all roofs, with additional inspections after major storms. Roofs over 10 years old may benefit from twice-yearly checks. Regular inspections catch small problems before they become expensive repairs.

What's the difference between an inspection and an estimate?

A roof inspection is a comprehensive assessment of your roof's condition, documenting all issues whether you repair them or not. An estimate focuses on specific known problems and provides pricing for repairs. Our inspections often include repair estimates when issues are found.

How quickly can you schedule an inspection?

Most non-emergency inspections can be scheduled within 3-5 business days. We offer same-day emergency inspections for urgent situations like storm damage or active leaks (additional fees apply). Post-storm demand may extend wait times.

How long does a roof inspection take?

Basic visual inspections take about 1 hour. Comprehensive inspections typically require 2-3 hours. Premium inspections with thermal imaging may take 3-4 hours. Duration depends on roof size, complexity, accessibility, and condition.

Will inspectors walk on my roof?

When safely accessible, yes. Our inspectors follow strict safety protocols and use appropriate equipment. For steep, fragile, or dangerous roofs, we use drone technology and ground-level assessment tools to avoid damage or injury risk.

What should I do to prepare for an inspection?

Ensure inspector access to property, clear driveway for parking, secure pets, provide attic access if needed, locate any previous inspection or repair records, and note any specific concerns you want addressed.
