Types of Asphalt Shingles We Install

Three-Tab Shingles

The most economical option, three-tab shingles provide a clean, uniform appearance. They're lightweight, easy to install, and perfect for budget-conscious homeowners who don't want to compromise on quality.

Architectural (Dimensional) Shingles

Our most popular choice, architectural shingles offer enhanced durability and a more sophisticated appearance with dimensional depth. They typically last 25-30 years and provide better wind and impact resistance than three-tab options.

Luxury (Designer) Shingles

For homeowners seeking premium aesthetics, luxury shingles replicate the look of slate, cedar shake, or tile roofing at a fraction of the cost. These high-performance shingles come with extended warranties and exceptional weather protection.

Impact-Resistant Shingles

Specially designed for areas prone to hail and severe weather, impact-resistant shingles can significantly reduce damage and may qualify you for insurance discounts.

Signs Your Asphalt Shingle Roof Needs Attention

Watch for these indicators that it's time to call {{business_name}}:

  • Shingles that are cracked, curled, or missing
  • Visible granule loss (check your gutters for accumulation)
  • Daylight visible through roof boards in the attic
  • Water stains on ceilings or walls
  • Sagging roof sections
  • Age of roof exceeding 20 years
  • Recent severe weather events
  • Higher than normal energy bills (poor insulation/ventilation)

Choosing the Right Shingle Color

The color of your asphalt shingles impacts both aesthetics and performance. Consider these factors:

  • Home Style: Match your shingles to your home's architectural style and existing colors
  • Climate: Lighter colors reflect heat in warm climates; darker colors absorb heat in cooler regions
  • Resale Value: Neutral colors appeal to the widest range of potential buyers
  • HOA Requirements: Check if your homeowner's association has color restrictions
  • Energy Efficiency: Cool-roof shingles can reduce cooling costs in hot climates
